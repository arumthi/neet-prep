# NEET 2026 Biology Crash Course

## Key Concepts

### 1. Diversity in Living World
*   **Classification of Organisms:** Five Kingdom Classification - Monera, Protista, Fungi, Plantae, Animalia.
*   **Plant Kingdom:** Algae, Bryophytes, Pteridophytes, Gymnosperms, Angiosperms.
*   **Animal Kingdom:** Invertebrates and Vertebrates.

### 2. Structural Organisation in Plants and Animals
*   **Morphology of Flowering Plants:** Root, Stem, Leaf, Flower, Fruit.
*   **Anatomy of Flowering Plants:** Tissues (Meristematic, Permanent), Tissue Systems (Epidermal, Ground, Vascular).
*   **Animal Tissues:** Epithelial, Connective, Muscular, Neural tissues.

### 3. Cell Structure and Function
*   **Cell: The Unit of Life:** Prokaryotic and Eukaryotic cells, Cell membrane, Cell wall, Nucleus, Cell organelles (Mitochondria, Chloroplasts, Endoplasmic Reticulum, Golgi apparatus).
*   **Biomolecules:** Carbohydrates, Proteins, Lipids, Nucleic acids (DNA, RNA), Enzymes.
*   **Cell Cycle and Cell Division:** Mitosis, Meiosis.

### 4. Plant Physiology
*   **Transport in Plants:** Transport of water and minerals, Osmosis, Diffusion.
*   **Mineral Nutrition:** Essential elements, Deficiency symptoms.
*   **Photosynthesis:** Light-dependent and light-independent reactions, C3 and C4 pathways.
*   **Respiration in Plants:** Glycolysis, Krebs cycle, Electron transport chain.
*   **Plant Growth and Development:** Auxins, Gibberellins, Cytokinins, Ethylene, Abscisic acid.

### 5. Human Physiology
*   **Digestion and Absorption:** Digestive system, Enzymes.
*   **Breathing and Exchange of Gases:** Respiratory system, Gas exchange.
*   **Body Fluids and Circulation:** Blood, Lymph, Structure and function of the heart.
*   **Excretory Products and their Elimination:** Structure and function of the kidney.
*   **Locomotion and Movement:** Skeleton, Muscles.
*   **Neural Control and Coordination:** Nervous system, Brain, Sense organs.
*   **Chemical Coordination and Integration:** Endocrine glands, Hormones.

### 6. Reproduction
*   **Reproduction in Organisms:** Asexual and Sexual reproduction.
*   **Sexual Reproduction in Flowering Plants:** Structure of flower, Pollination, Fertilization, Seed and fruit development.
*   **Human Reproduction:** Male and female reproductive systems, Fertilization, Embryonic development.
*   **Reproductive Health:** Family planning, Sexually transmitted diseases.

### 7. Genetics and Evolution
*   **Principles of Inheritance and Variation:** Mendel's laws, Chromosomal theory, Genetic disorders.
*   **Molecular Basis of Inheritance:** DNA structure, Replication, Transcription, Translation, Genetic code, Human Genome Project.
*   **Evolution:** Origin of life, Theories of evolution (Darwin), Human evolution.

### 8. Biology and Human Welfare
*   **Human Health and Diseases:** Common diseases, Immunity, Cancer, AIDS.
*   **Strategies for Enhancement in Food Production:** Plant and animal breeding.
*   **Microbes in Human Welfare:** Household products, Industrial production, Sewage treatment.

### 9. Biotechnology
*   **Biotechnology: Principles and Processes:** Genetic engineering, Recombinant DNA technology.
*   **Biotechnology and its Applications:** Medicine, Agriculture.

### 10. Ecology
*   **Organisms and Populations:** Ecological factors, Population interactions.
*   **Ecosystem:** Structure and function, Energy flow, Nutrient cycling.
*   **Biodiversity and Conservation:** Loss of biodiversity, Conservation strategies.
*   **Environmental Issues:** Pollution, Global warming.

## Sample Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

**1. Oxygen is released during photosynthesis from which of the following?**
   a) Carbon dioxide
   b) Water
   c) Glucose
   d) ATP

   **Answer:** b) Water

**2. Who proposed the double helix model of DNA?**
   a) Mendel
   b) Darwin
   c) Watson and Crick
   d) Lamarck

   **Answer:** c) Watson and Crick

**3. Which is the largest endocrine gland in the human body?**
   a) Pituitary
   b) Thyroid
   c) Adrenal
   d) Pancreas

   **Answer:** b) Thyroid

**4. How does energy flow in an ecosystem?**
   a) Cyclically
   b) Unidirectionally
   c) Bidirectionally
   d) Unstable

   **Answer:** b) Unidirectionally

**5. Which is the key enzyme used in genetic engineering?**
   a) Amylase
   b) Lipase
   c) Restriction endonuclease
   d) Pepsin

   **Answer:** c) Restriction endonuclease
